# Introduction

## What is Beyond the Veil?

Beyond the Veil (Veil) is a high-fantasy tabletop RPG featuring tactical action-based combat, simultaneous turns, and flexible horizontal progression. Players build characters without restriction; the game contains no classic ability scores or fiddly modifiers. Instead, challenges use a Difficulty Check (DC) and outcomes resolve across **four degrees of success**. Rolls do not use additional dice or small situational modifiers. Players shape characters through Masteries, Skills, and Equipment, choosing from tactical and narrative options within a **3-action economy with 1 Reaction**. Magic and other powers are represented as abilities within masteries or skills rather than strict spell lists; any character who meets the prerequisites can learn them. Veil prioritizes roleplaying and tactical choice over tracking character sheet modifiers and minute statistics.

# Creator’s Words

Veil focuses on character build options rather than modifier chasing. As characters gain experience and grow in power, they unlock new options to solve problems instead of inflating numerical statistics. Choose tools that achieve your goals rather than those that simply increase die results. Settings in Veil use words like magic, arcane, and mana. The game assumes a world where all creatures control mana and the supernatural is a natural part of daily life. Inhabitants develop their mana control as an everyday skill, much like physical training.

# Main Resolution Mechanic

To resolve actions, make a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Veil Roll\|Veil Roll]]: roll a d20 against a Difficulty Class (DC). Every roll matters. Outcomes use four degrees of success. Mastery ranks gate power and options across three tiers: Adept, Veteran, and Master. In Veil, always round fractions down. The roll measures the degree of success or failure. For example, when attacking with a weapon, the roll determines damage effectiveness directly rather than resolving hits and damage rolls separately.

## 4 Degrees of Success and Failure

When making a Veil Roll against a DC, rolling 10 or more above the DC is a critical success. Rolling 10 or more below the DC is a critical failure. A natural 20 increases the degree of success by one step, while a natural 1 reduces it by one step. Apply the natural 20 or 1 adjustment to the d20 result after adding modifiers, then compare the final total to the DC to determine the final degree. To maximize success and avoid failure, party members must coordinate and assist one another. Because modifiers are scarce, every bonus is significant.

## **Boons and Banes**

Modify Veil rolls with [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon\|Boon]] and [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane\|Bane]] effects. Boons provide positive modifiers, while Banes impose penalties or harmful conditions. Both can be minor or major. A minor Boon or Bane adds or subtracts 2 from the roll, while a major [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon\|Boon]] or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane\|Bane]] adds or subtracts 4. Boons and Banes apply to specific rolls as defined by their providing feature, condition, or ability.

### Boon and Bane stacking

When multiple boons and banes affect a single roll, apply the following rules:
- A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon\|Boon]] or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane\|Bane]] adds or subtracts 2.
- A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Major Boon\|Major Boon]] or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Major Bane\|Major Bane]] adds or subtracts 4.
- The maximum total modifier for any roll is +4 or -4, regardless of the number of active boons or banes.
- Boons and banes are additive:
    - E.g., A major boon (+4) and a bane (-2) result in a net +2 modifier.
    - E.g., 2 [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Major Bane\|Major Bane]] (-8) and one [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon\|Boon]] (+2) result in a net -6 modifier, the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane\|Bane]] is set to -4 due to the cap of 4.

### **Open Rolls**
Veil rolls are open. The only secret rolls are those designated by the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Secret\|Secret]] trait.

# **Heroes in Veil**
To build a character, select a species or sub-species, choose two origins, and pick four knowledge topics. Alternatively, create a custom origin. Every character starts with at least one known language. These choices establish your character's background, which you can expand with a written backstory.

## [[Species\|Species]]

Choose a species to define your character's size, silhouette, and flavor. Species carries no mechanical restrictions on capabilities.

## [[Origins\|Origins]]

Origins define your character's past residence, education, and profession. Origins grant starting knowledge and minor progression unlocks. All characters start with 10 [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Wealth\|Wealth]], standard clothing, and a [[Backpack\|Backpack]].

## **Unique Aspect & Backstory**

Your character’s Unique Aspect is a custom feature that distinguishes them from everyone else in the setting. This trait provides narrative flavor and helps the Director tie your character to the story. Unique Aspects must not provide combat advantages; their purpose is to inspire story arcs and roleplay.

## Character Progression
Character progression expands your options rather than inflating numbers. You unlock stronger abilities and find powerful magical gear, but core statistics—such as [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]], weapon damage, and ability damage—remain flat across all tiers of play. This rule applies to both characters and monsters.

## Tiers of Play
Play is divided into three tiers: Adept, Veteran, and Master. These tiers measure your party's experience, renown, and training.

- [[Adept\|Adept]]: Adepts are fresh but competent adventurers. When local guards cannot handle a threat, Adepts step in.
- [[Veteran\|Veteran]]: Veterans possess established allies, magical gear, and renown. Leaders and kings call on Veterans for national threats.
- [[Master\|Master]]: Masters are legendary figures of unmatched skill. Masters resolve world-ending threats beyond the reach of common mortals.

# Experience Points (XP)

Characters start with 28 XP (28 Mastery XP and 28 Skill XP). The Director can adjust this starting value to suit the campaign style. XP is split into Mastery XP and Skill XP. Earning XP grants equal points to both pools, but you spend them separately to develop your character in different directions. You earn XP by finding secrets, overcoming challenges, defeating dangerous creatures, and negotiating with NPCs. XP functions as a point-buy system. You can only purchase options available within your current tier of play (Adept, Veteran, or Master).

- Trivial challenges yield 0 XP.
- Risky challenges yield 1 XP.
- Highly dangerous challenges yield 2 XP.
- Extreme challenges yield 3 or 4 XP.

Spend Mastery XP during character creation or downtime to purchase Masteries (Disciplines and Equipment), Abilities, and Signature Abilities. Spend Skill XP to purchase Skill Proficiencies and Skill Features.

| Item                                 | XP Cost |
| ------------------------------------ | ------- |
| Discipline or Equipment Mastery      | 4       |
| Ability or Signature Ability Upgrade | 2       |
| Skill Proficiency or Skill Feature   | 4       |

## Choose Your Defenses

Allocate one **Strong (+4)**, one **Moderate (+2)**, and one **Weak (+0)** bonus among [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Fortitude\|Fortitude]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Reflex\|Reflex]], and [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Will\|Will]]. Each base value is 10 before allocation.

# Masteries and Skills

Masteries and Skills represent your character's training, gear proficiencies, and expertise. These are the core building blocks for combat, social interaction, and exploration.

### Masteries and Skill Buying (Unlock)

Spending XP on a Mastery (such as Stormweaver or Swords) unlocks it. You can then spend additional XP within that Mastery to learn abilities or upgrade them to signature abilities. Upon entering a new Tier of Play, you can upgrade known Masteries to the next tier to access advanced options. Ranks must be unlocked sequentially (e.g., you must unlock Veteran Swords before unlocking Master Swords).

When you purchase a Skill Proficiency, your Veil rolls for that skill (such as Subterfuge) gain advantage (roll 2d20 and take the highest). Proficiency unlocks corresponding skill features. Unlike Masteries, you can purchase skill features from higher tiers as soon as you enter those tiers, without upgrading the proficiency itself.

You cannot spend XP on Masteries you have not unlocked, or on Skills in which you lack proficiency.

### Mastery & Skill Limits

Strict limits govern your maximum number of known Masteries and Skill Proficiencies. These limits expand with each tier of play. Once you reach your limit, you cannot learn more Masteries or Skill Proficiencies, though you can still learn new abilities and skill features within unlocked trees.

| Tier    | Mastery Limit | Skill Proficiency Limit |
| ------- | ------------- | ----------------------- |
| Adept   | 4             | 3                       |
| Veteran | 5             | 4                       |
| Master  | 6             | 5                       |

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery\|Mastery]] 

A mastery represents specialized training, practice, or innate talent with specific abilities or equipment.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Discipline Mastery\|Discipline Mastery]]

Discipline Mastery represents a trained school of martial, magical, or mixed techniques (e.g., Pyromancy, Berserker, Vanguard). Your rank (Adept, Veteran, or Master) gates which abilities and passive effects you can learn. Spend XP to learn abilities within your Disciplines or to increase the Discipline's rank. Each Discipline grants a unique active or passive feature that improves as you invest XP.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Equipment Mastery\|Equipment Mastery]] 

Equipment Mastery defines your proficiency with weapons, armor, and specialized gear. Spending XP in Equipment Masteries unlocks abilities and passive traits. These abilities require you to wield or wear the corresponding equipment to activate them.

#### Equipment Families

Equipment Masteries apply to families of items (e.g., Swords) and specific types (e.g., Short Sword, Long Sword). Equipment Mastery applies to Type, while runes and certain abilities target Family. Types within a Family share a core identity (such as damage type, basic attacks, and critical effects) but differ in hands, reach, and traits. Weapons belonging to multiple families can use abilities from both masteries.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Companion Mastery\|Companion Mastery]] 

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Companion Mastery\|Companion Mastery]] grants control of a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Companion\|Companion]] creature. Spending XP in this mastery increases your companion's strength, grants them new traits, and unlocks cooperative abilities.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Core Mastery\|Core Mastery]] 

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Core Mastery\|Core Mastery]] provides a single defining, rule-breaking feature with a limited set of abilities. Core Masteries support and enhance other masteries.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Signature\|Signature]] Abilities

Starting at the Veteran tier, you can spend XP to designate an ability as a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Signature\|Signature]] ability, allowing you to enhance or alter its effects. When using a Signature ability, you can apply at most **one** signature effect, or choose to apply none.

## Skill Proficiency

When unlocking a skill proficiency, your Veil Rolls for said skill check roll 2d20s and you take the highest roll (This is called Advantage). For skills which you are not proficient in, you roll a single d20.

## Skill Features

As you gain more skill features by spending XP,  you open new possibilities, learn skill abilities and also gain passive benefits related to your proficient skills.

## Skill Checks

**Skill Checks** occur only when failure carries a consequence or risk. Static checks against the environment use fixed DCs that do not scale with the party. Checks against other creatures use DCs derived from the target's statistics.

- DC 4 Simple
- DC 8 Standard
- DC 12 Tricky
- DC 16 Difficult
- DC 20 Extreme
- Impossible (no rolling)

### Abilities as Skill Checks

Certain abilities can bypass or assist skill checks. In these cases, the Director grants an automatic success or treats the check as if it received a successful Help action.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Group Skill Check\|Group Skill Check]] 

When an event calls for a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Group Skill Check\|Group Skill Check]], you (the party) all roll against 1 or more DCs, and each success grants ‘points’ towards a success. Each failure grants a failure point. At the end of the skill challenge, if the number of successes is equal or higher than failure, you succeed.

| **Degree**       | **Effect**           |
| ---------------- | -------------------- |
| Critical Success | You gain 2 successes |
| Success          | You gain 1 success   |
| Failure          | You gain a failure   |
| Critical Failure | You gain 2 failures  |
### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Skirmish\|Skirmish]] 

A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Skirmish\|Skirmish]] is a high-level combat [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Group Skill Check\|Group Skill Check]] used to resolve minor encounters without entering Encounter Mode. The party is guaranteed to win; rolls measure performance and consequences.

A Skirmish has a set DC. The Director may lower this DC if the party describes a particularly suitable approach (such as charging with [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Athletics\|Athletics]] or ambushing with [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Subterfuge\|Subterfuge]]).

Example:

- A group of bandits are aware of the you. You can charge in [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Athletics\|Athletics]] or surprise them [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Subterfuge\|Subterfuge]].
- A group of assassins surprise you, you roll [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Perception\|Perception]] on the chance spotting them, or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Agility\|Agility]] to react quickly.
- A pack of wolves have surrounded the you, you can use [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Smarts\|Smarts]] to create a strategy on the fly, or growl back to scare them into breaking formation using [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Influence\|Influence]].

On a success the party defeats their opponents easily, on a failure, the party defeats the opponents easily but the [[luck\|luck]] die is rolled. If you roll a critical failure (personal roll), you also gain a Reaper’s Mark representing a blunder on your side or the opponents getting a lucky shot on you.

| **Degree**       | **Effect**                                                    |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Critical Success | You gain 2 successes                                          |
| Success          | You gain 1 success                                            |
| Failure          | You gain a failure                                            |
| Critical Failure | You gain 2 failures, and you personally gain 1 Reaper’s Mark. |

### Secret Checks

Skill checks, abilities or anything with the Secret Traits signifies that the Director makes the Veil Roll without revealing the result, they only narrate the outcome.

### Skill Challenges

Skill challenges are a special set of group skills checks that are run as a group. These challenges are used in cases where you have to complete a set number of obstacles, movement traps, without going into encounter mode. Most of the skill challenges are run in Exploration Mode.

# [[Campcraft\|Campcraft]]

During rests or extended downtime, you use **Campcraft** to manage adventuring logistics. Here, players spend wealth to unlock, purchase, and modify gear, tools, kits, and consumables.

### **Wealth**

**Wealth** abstracts the party's valuables into a single unit. Use Wealth to purchase gear via Campcraft or sway NPCs. Routine living expenses are ignored; the Director demands Wealth only for exceptional costs (such as luxury lodging, feasts, or bribes).

*Wealth is abstract. One unit of Wealth represents a bag of 10 gold pieces, a bundle of valuable furs, or a piece of jewelry. The party has carrying limits for Wealth.*

**The Director determines if a transaction requires Wealth.**

### **Inventory Management**

Inventory management is shared; the party shares a single inventory space to purchase and store items.

### Consumables

Purchasing a consumable recipe allows you to completely refill that consumable's stack during rests. Downtime actions can temporarily increase this stock. For example, purchasing the Healing Potion recipe ensures you have 4 Healing Potions after every rest, stored in the shared Backpack or distributed among players.

Different consumables have unique stack sizes. Campcraft and downtime actions allow you to expand and modify these items.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Loadout\|Loadout]]

Characters start with 6 Loadout slots. The Loadout holds items, weapons, or gear designated for quick access. Weapons and shields must be in your Loadout to be used; held items count against your Loadout limit. Loadout is vital during [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]].

Secondary weapons, potions, wands, trinkets, and bombs must be in your Loadout to be used without action penalties.

Retrieving and using an item from the Backpack requires a 3-action [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Actions/Backpack interaction\|Backpack interaction]]. If the item requires multiple actions to activate, this interaction leaves you holding the item.

Items with a stack number (such as potions) occupy 1 Loadout slot per individual item (e.g., carrying 2 Stamina Potions requires 2 Loadout slots).

### [[Backpack\|Backpack]]

The [[Backpack\|Backpack]] is the party’s shared inventory. It holds supplies, tools, camping gear, backup equipment, and magical items. Players do not need to track individual carrying ownership. Stackable items occupy only 1 Backpack slot per stack. The party starts with 20 shared Backpack slots.

Separately, the party has 20 slots dedicated specifically to Wealth. This tracks the amount of physical treasure, loot, and coinage the party can carry. Spend wealth rather than hoarding it. Upgrading the Backpack increases both Backpack slots.

When Backpack or Wealth limits are reached, the party cannot carry additional items or treasure.

### Cargo

Cargo is additional storage at your campsite, representing items not carried during active exploration. Access [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cargo\|Cargo]] during a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Rest\|Rest]].

### Gear Slots ([[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn\|Worn]] Items)

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn\|Worn]] items are equipped on your person and do not require hands to use. They may require an [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Actions/Interact\|Interact]] action or a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Strike\|Strike]] to activate. Worn [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Weapons/Weapons\|Weapons]] require a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Loadout\|Loadout]] slot. Worn categories include [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boots\|Boots]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Eyewear\|Eyewear]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Headwear\|Headwear]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cape\|Cape]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Gloves\|Gloves]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Chestpiece\|Chestpiece]], and [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Belt\|Belt]].

## Equipment

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Weapons/Weapons\|Weapons]] have a fixed Damage value that does not scale with level. They list Basic Traits (defining core properties) and Custom Traits (unlocked by tier and swappable during downtime crafting). At Veteran rank, each weapon mastery unlocks a unique Modification.

### Weapon Variety

Weapons are grouped by Family (e.g., [[Swords\|Swords]]) and Type (e.g., [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Weapons/Greatsword\|Greatsword]]). Equipment Mastery applies to Type, while runes and certain abilities target Family. Types within a Family share a core identity (such as damage type, basic attacks, and critical effects) but differ in hands, reach, and traits. Weapons belonging to multiple families can use abilities from both masteries.

### **Protection**

Standard protective clothing sets your Defense to 12; its appearance is purely narrative. You can invest in Armor, Wards, or Vestment Equipment Masteries to upgrade this into specialized protective gear with unique traits.

## Magical Items

Magical gear provides significant power as the party defeats monsters, clears dungeons, and completes quests. Magical items vary in power, usage, and duration.

### Shards

Shards are unstable, single-use magical items. Activating a shard requires rolling a d4. On a 1, the shard is permanently destroyed after resolving.

### Exalted

Exalted items possess powerful magic, granting significant bonuses to a soulbound wielder.

### Runes

Runes enhance other objects, primarily weapons and armor. You can add, remove, or replace runes during downtime. Runes are restricted to compatible gear (e.g., a slashing rune cannot be applied to a bow).

### Artifacts

Artifacts represent the pinnacle of magical power. Each artifact is unique, reality-bending, and scales in power with the soulbound wielder across play tiers.

### **Soulbinding**

To activate and channel a magic item's effects, you must perform a Soulbinding. A character can soulbind up to 6 magical items simultaneously.

# Knowledge

Knowledge represents the sum of topics you understand rather than an active skill. When a topic is relevant, make a Knowledge check. Because knowledge domains are broad, the Director allows flexible connections between topics.

- **Basic Knowledge**
    - Common information known to local inhabitants. This never requires a roll.
- **Domain Knowledge**
    - Mastery of a broad topic (e.g., Undead). This requires a Knowledge check to recall specific details.
- **Specialized Knowledge**
    - Expertise in a narrow subset of a domain (e.g., Wolves within Mammals). Specialized Knowledge checks gain Advantage.

The Director categorizes information based on the setting.

## **Knowledge Checks**

The Director rolls **Knowledge Checks** in secret. These checks occur only for complex information outside common domain knowledge, and are limited to one attempt per character per topic.

Knowledge checks are generous. If multiple domains apply, you gain a Minor Boon for each relevant topic. For example, having both mountain lore and mammal lore grants a Boon when identifying mountain-dwelling beasts.

| **Degree**       | **Effect**                                                |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Critical Success | You know **everything** there is to know about the topic. |
| Success          | You know **plenty** about this topic.                     |
| Failure          | You know** little** about this topic.                     |
| Critical Failure | You know **nothing** about this topic.                    |

### Knowledge Checks about Creatures or Monsters

Knowledge checks about monsters apply to the general species rather than a specific individual.

| **Degree** | **Effect** |
| --- | --- |
| Critical Success | You know the creature’s abilities (all of them), behavior, movement types, habitat, general lore, and any boons or banes. |
| Success | You know the creature’s main ability, behavior, movement types, habitat, and general lore. |
| Failure | You know general lore about the creature. |
| Critical Failure | You know nothing about the creature. |

## **Learning and gaining Knowledge**

Adventurers expand their knowledge through study and experience.

### Knowledge Capacity

Basic knowledge and languages do not count against your capacity. Each known [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Knowledge\|Knowledge]] domain or specialized topic adds 1 to your knowledge capacity. This capacity serves as the DC for [[Learning\|Learning]] checks. Unlike other DCs in Veil, the Learning DC is determined solely by your current capacity.

### Learning new things

To learn a new topic, you must acquire study material, study during downtime, and succeed on a check.

### Study Material

Learning requires appropriate study material, such as a book, a codex, a magical item, or a tutor. The Director determines if your materials and conditions are sufficient.

### Learning Downtime

To learn a domain, spend a downtime action studying. Roll a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Skill Check/Smarts Skill Check\|Smarts Skill Check]] plus learning bonuses. If the total meets or exceeds your current Knowledge DC, you learn the domain. On a failure, you gain a stacking Deep Study +2 bonus for subsequent attempts.

| Degree           | Effect                                               |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------- |
| Success          | You gain the Domain as part of your known knowledge. |
| Failure          | You gain Deep Study +2                               |
| Critical Failure | None                                                 |

### Deep Study

Failing a Learning Roll grants a stacking +2 bonus to subsequent rolls for that specific Domain, ensuring eventual success.

# Resources

Veil features minimal resource tracking, keeping the party near full power during exploration. However, accumulated injuries require a full rest to recover.

### **Cooldowns**

Abilities have a cooldown of one use by default, requiring you to [[Take a Break\|Take a Break]] to use them again. Rituals and specialized abilities require a full day to recharge.

### **Consumables**

Unlocked Campcraft consumables replenish automatically during rests. Consumables purchased or found in the world are permanently consumed upon use.

## Death

### Reaper’s Marks

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Reaper’s Mark\|Reaper’s Mark]]s track how close a hero is to permanent death, representing wounds, injuries, and trauma. Each hero has a maximum of 6 Reaper's Marks; reaching 6 results in permanent death.

- Reaching 0 [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]] immediately inflicts 1 Reaper's Mark. Any further damage taken while at 0 Vitality inflicts an additional Reaper's Mark.
- Taking damage equal to double your maximum [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]] results in instant death ([[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Fatality\|Fatality]]).
- Taking damage equal to or exceeding your maximum [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]] (but less than double) knocks you [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Conditions/Unconscious\|Unconscious]] ([[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Colossal Blow\|Colossal Blow]]).
- Rests reduce your Reaper's Marks by 2. Skills, consumables, and abilities can accelerate this recovery.
- Resting in a dangerous dungeon reduces Reaper's Marks but does not automatically clear them.

### Hazards

Outside of combat, characters remain at full vitality. Because traps are designed to mutilate or kill, they deal direct Reaper's Marks instead of Vitality damage. Complex traps and hazards resolve in [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]].

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]] resolves combat and complex hazards where every action has immediate impact. Play is structured in rounds and turns. A turn is a single creature's window of action. A round concludes once all participants have taken their turns. Faction turn order is determined by [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative\|Initiative]].

### **Action Economy**

### Actions & Reactions

At the start of your turn, you gain 3 actions and 1 reaction.

[[Universal Actions\|Universal Actions]]: Basic actions available to all characters without cooldowns, provided prerequisites are met.

[[Mastery Actions\|Mastery Actions]]: Specialized actions unlocked via specific Skills or Masteries.

### **Attacking**

Attacks (any action or ability with the Attack trait) require a Veil Roll, adding Prowess and applying any boons or banes against the target's [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Defense\|Defense]].

### Attacking and Traits

Attacks automatically inherit the traits of their damage types. For example, an axe attack dealing additional fire damage gains both the Slashing and Fire traits.

### Attack Types

- **Attack** - Roll Prowess against the target’s [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Defense\|Defense]].
    - Example: Firing a fire bolt at a wolf (Defense 8). A Prowess check of 8 or higher is a success.
- **Strike** - An attack using a held weapon or unarmed combat. This applies all weapon traits and properties. Roll Prowess against the target’s [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Defense\|Defense]].
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power Attack\|Power Attack]] - An attack that deals half damage on a failure.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power Strike\|Power Strike]] - A strike that deals half damage on a failure, using a weapon.
- **Overpower** - An [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Overpower/Overpower\|Overpower]] attack targets the defender's [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Defense\|Defense]], but also targets their [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Fortitude\|Fortitude]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Reflex\|Reflex]], or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Will\|Will]]. Use the attack roll's total for both checks. Overpower triggers only if the target takes [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]] damage. If a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Barrier\|Barrier]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Actions/Shield Block\|Shield Block]], or damage reduction negates the damage, Overpower does not apply.
- **Prowess rolls that target Fortitude, Reflex or Will** - Certain abilities, like [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Actions/Shove\|Shove]], target [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Fortitude\|Fortitude]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Reflex\|Reflex]], or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Will\|Will]] directly using a the Prowess roll.
    - Example: Shoving a wolf (Fortitude 10). A Prowess check of 10 or higher succeeds.
- **Unarmed Attacks** - Unarmed attacks use punches, kicks, headbutts, or tails. Without Adept training in unarmed combat, you deal 1 Crushing Damage and cannot Engage opponents.

### **Abilities**

Abilities define your tactical options, acquired primarily by spending Mastery XP and Skill XP.

### Basic Defenses

When a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Prowess\|Prowess]] roll targets one of the three [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Basic Defenses\|Basic Defenses]], apply damage and effects based on the degree of success:

| Degree           | Effect        |
| ---------------- | ------------- |
| Critical Success | Double Damage |
| Success          | Full Damage   |
| Failure          | Half Damage   |
| Critical Failure | No Effect     |

### **Dealing Damage**

Weapons and abilities define the flat damage value and damage type. Damage does not roll for variation; instead, the degree of success on the attack roll determines the final damage.

### Calculating Damage (order)

Determine damage based on the roll result. Calculate the final value for **each damage type individually** using these steps:

- Normal Damage
    1. Base: Apply the listed Damage value.
    2. Modifiers: Add any [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/additional damage\|additional damage]] effects.
    3. Banes and Boons: Apply relevant defender boons or banes to each damage type individually.
- Half Damage
    1. Base: Apply the listed Damage value.
    2. Modifiers: Add any [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/additional damage\|additional damage]] effects.
    3. Halve: Halve each damage type individually.
    4. Banes and Boons: Apply defender boons or banes to each damage type individually.
- Critical Damage Calculation
    1. Base: Apply the listed Damage value.
    2. Multiply: Double the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Base Damage\|Base Damage]].
    3. Modifiers: Add any [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/additional damage\|additional damage]] effects.
    4. Critical Effect: Apply any additional [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Critical Effect\|Critical Effect]].
    5. Banes and Boons: Apply defender boons or banes to each damage type individually.

### Damage Immunity

Immunity negates all damage of that specific type.

### Boon against damage

A boon to a damage type reduces damage taken of that type by 2 (minor boon) or 4 (major boon). This can reduce damage to 0. Apply boons to each damage type individually.

### Bane against Damage

A bane to a damage type increases damage taken of that type by 2 (minor bane) or 4 (major bane).

### Lethal and Non-Lethal Damage

Attacks are lethal by default. Declaring a non-lethal attack reduces your degree of success by one step. Weapons or abilities with the Non-Lethal trait deal normal damage but resolve as non-lethal without penalty.

### Critical Damage and Critical Effect

Critical success doubles the base damage. Additional, rider, or imbued damage is not doubled unless specified. Critical effects resolve after calculating critical damage.

## **Area of Effect**

Areas of effect are defined by shape and distance. Grid play uses square measurements rather than circles.

### Burst

Measured as a square (e.g., a 5x5 meter burst). Range is measured from the center of the burst.

### Emanation

An area originating from and centered on a creature or entity.

### Line

Defined by length and width, originating from the user (e.g., a 10x2 meter line).

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cone\|Cone]]

Cones are defined by length. On a grid, a cone starts at 1 square adjacent to the user and expands by 2 squares in width for every meter of length.

## **Tactical**

### Engaging

Standing in an **adjacent** space with a creature means you are **Engaged** with them. If an ally also engages that creature, the creature becomes Exposed, easing subsequent attacks.

You can only engage a creature if:
- You are adjacent.
- The target can see you.
- You are conscious and active.
- You wield a melee weapon (reach weapons do not engage unless you are adjacent) or are trained in unarmed combat.

### Cover

Positioning behind protective obstacles allows you to use the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Actions/Take Cover\|Take Cover]] action to gain [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cover\|Cover]].

When targeted while in cover, roll **1d6** after the attack roll. A success shifts the attack's degree of success one step in your favor (e.g., Critical Success to Success, Success to Failure):
- **Light cover** (low walls, foliage, intervening creatures): Succeeds on a **6**.
- **Standard cover** (half walls, doorframes): Succeeds on a **5–6**.
- **Heavy cover** (arrow slits, murder holes): Succeeds on a **4–6**.
- **Total cover**: Prevents targeting entirely.

Cover is relative to each attacker and requires a partially blocked line of effect.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative\|Initiative]]

Veil uses side-based initiative. When Encounter Mode begins, the player characters take their turns first, followed by the NPCs. In an ambush, the ambushing side acts first. You can use an [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative Action\|Initiative Action]] and take your turn!

### Turns and Rounds

The party acts simultaneously during their turn, executing moves, attacks, and abilities in any order. Once all party members finish, the opposing side acts. Abilities with the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Combo\|Combo]] trait allow simultaneous action coordination. A round ends when all factions have completed their turns.

### Delaying

You can delay your turn, allowing another faction to act first.

### Ambush

If one side completely surprises another, the ambushers act first. Ambushees cannot use [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative Action\|Initiative Action]]s. The ambush continues until an action reveals the ambushers or the defenders detect the threat.

### Multi-Faction Battles

In battles with three or more factions, play alternates between factions based on who initiated the conflict.

### **Combat Timer**

Combat is designed for rapid decision-making. Each round should conclude in under 10 minutes. Players and the Director must declare actions and resolve rolls within this limit. Unresolved actions are lost when the timer expires.

# [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] structures time in flexible blocks. Players choose passive or active activities for their characters as they travel, search, scout, or sneak. Quick exploration activities take 10 minutes; complex tasks require hours or days. Available activities are listed in [[Exploration Activities\|Exploration Activities]].

Exploration Mode uses three structures: Passive Activities, Active Activities, and Dungeon Turns (time segments).

- **Passive Activities**: Define ongoing behavior, such as movement speed, searching for traps, or scouting for ambushes.
- **Active Activities**: Occur when the party performs a specific task like a ritual. The party must declare their actions for the next 10 minutes.
- **Movement**: The group moves at the speed of the slowest member. The group can select one shared Movement activity for free.

If combat begins during exploration, the Director determines how many rounds of action are required to resume or complete the exploration activity.

Taking a 10-minute break ([[Take a Break\|Take a Break]]) recovers ability and item cooldowns and restores characters to full vitality.

## Doubling Up

If two characters perform the same exploration activity, both roll and the party uses the higher result.

## Backtracking

The party can bypass exploration checks and walk normally when backtracking through cleared areas.

### **Push Your Luck (Luck)**

To track time and environmental hazard, Veil uses a Luck system. If the party takes too long or makes noise, they must test their Luck.

### How You Use It

The Luck die starts as a d20. Each roll degrades the die to the next lower size (d20 > d12 > d10 > d8 > d6 > d4 > d2). Rolling a natural 1 triggers a Complication and resets the Luck die to a d20.

### Spending Time

Roll the Luck die whenever the party performs a slow, methodical, or complex action taking 10 minutes or more (such as searching a room, resting, repairing gear, or identifying an item).

### Recklessness and Failure

Rushed, loud, or risky actions require rolling the Luck die with disadvantage (roll twice and use the lower result).

### Extreme Risks

Extremely dangerous actions (such as resting in active monster territory or causing loud echoes) immediately trigger a Luck roll using a d2.

### Complications

A Complication represents a negative environmental shift, resource loss, or alert patrol, rather than a direct combat encounter.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Social Mode\|Social Mode]] 

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Social Mode\|Social Mode]] resolves negotiations, interrogations, and debates. It uses free-flowing exchanges without initiative, reactions, or action economies. The Director guides the spotlight as both sides make conversational moves.

Social Mode uses four degrees of success and tracks two visible clocks: **Favor** (progress toward your goal) and **Backlash** (progress toward diplomatic failure). Social checks are Secret rolls by default.

### **The Four Affinities**

NPCs have specific conversational affinities. Player attempts target these affinities, which have varying DCs depending on the NPC's traits:

1. **Deception** - misdirection, lies, and contradictions.
2. **Intimidation** - aggression, pressure, and implied force.
3. **Diplomacy** - street smarts, terms, and compromises.
4. **Charm** - persuasion, rapport, and flattery.

### **Skills and Knowledge in Social Mode**

Describe your actions to determine which skill check applies. Each skill can be used only once per social encounter. Relevant specialized knowledge can replace skill checks and may lower the DC.

### **When to use Social Mode?**

The Director initiates Social Mode for high-stakes, multi-sided conversations, revealing the Favor and Backlash clocks. The scene ends immediately when either clock fills.

### **Favor & Backlash**

Social checks advance the clocks based on success:
- **Critical Success:** +2 Favor
- **Success:** +1 Favor
- **Failure:** +1 Backlash
- **Critical Failure:** +2 Backlash

### **Close the Veil**

During Social Mode, the Director may "Close the Veil," requiring players to minimize meta-discussion and speak in character.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Downtime Mode\|Downtime Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Downtime Mode\|Downtime Mode]] resolves rest and recovery. Setting up camp or staying at an inn grants downtime, during which characters perform [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Downtime Activity\|Downtime Activity]] options linked to Campcraft.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Rest\|Rest]]

You can rest only once every 24 hours. While you travel, simply sleeping and eating does not constitute as a rest, which allows all your preparations, cooldowns and durations to persistent for multiple days. When you activate a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Rest\|Rest]], you choose to take a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Downtime Rest\|Downtime Rest]] or a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Quick Rest\|Quick Rest]].

### **Quick Rest (6 hours)**

- Replenishes unlocked Campcraft consumables and replaces lost or broken basic gear.
- Recovers 2 Reaper's Marks.

### **Downtime Rest (12 hours)**

- Grants all benefits of a Quick Rest.
- Allows characters to perform 2 downtime actions.

### **Rest (In a Safe Haven)**

When you take a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Downtime Rest\|Downtime Rest]] in a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Safehaven\|Safehaven]] (such as a secure town or civilized settlement) you can spend wealth to unlock [[Campcraft\|Campcraft]] options, buy new equipment, in addition to standard rest benefits. Rests in hostile settlements do not grant Safehaven benefits.

### **Preparation**

Following a rest, the party enters a preparation phase to soulbind items, prepare consumables, and organize gear.

## General Rules

### **Mastery Distribution**

Character rank rarity in the general population:
- Adept: 1 in 1,000
- Veteran: 1 in 10,000
- Master: 1 in 10,000,000

### **Flat Checks**

A Flat Check is a standard Veil Roll without any modifiers.

### **Holding Gear**

### Hands

Weapons and items require [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/One Handed\|One Handed]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Two Handed\|Two Handed]], or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/1+ Hand\|1+ Hand]] grips. Releasing a grip or dropping an item is a free action. Activating loadout items requires an [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Actions/Interact\|Interact]] action.

### Reloading

Reloading a weapon requires a free hand.

### **Duration**

Standard durations include:
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Start of target’s next turn\|Start of target’s next turn]]: Expires at the start of the target's next turn.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/End of their next turn\|End of their next turn]]: Expires at the end of the target's next turn.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/End of Encounter\|End of Encounter]]: Expires when the encounter concludes. Outside of combat, this duration equals 1 minute.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Flat Check End\|Flat Check End]]: Requires a Flat Check at the end of your turn to determine if the effect persists.
- Sustained: Requires spending an action each turn to maintain the effect.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power of the Veil\|Power of the Veil]] 

Veil Points (VP) are a meta-currency used to influence the narrative.

### Veil Points Basics

- Characters start each session with 1 VP (maximum capacity of 1).
- Spend 1 VP to bypass an ability's standard cooldown.
- **Stretch Your Powers**: Spend 1 VP outside of combat to use an ability in a creative, non-standard way. The Director determines the outcome.
- **Deus Ex Machina**: Spend 1 VP to retroactively prepare for an unforeseen hazard or establish a narrative connection.
- **Will into existence**: Spend 1 VP to establish the presence of a reasonable, helpful environmental feature (such as a hay bale to soften a fall).
- **Devil’s Bargain**: If you have 0 VP, you can request a VP from the Director in exchange for a Complication or future narrative obligation.

### Light

Light sources project light as an emanation. Areas of Dim Light make targets [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Conditions/Concealed\|concealed]].

### **Crafting (Optional)**

Crafting is an optional system for creating specific magical gear instead of relying on random loot. Crafting projects have three phases: obtaining a blueprint, making progress, and finishing the item. Crafting requires significant time and wealth.

A blueprint specifies the required crafting points, necessary skills, required tools, and material costs.

- Each crafting downtime action costs 1 Wealth.
- You can progress a project without its required unique components, but you cannot complete it until you obtain them.
- Roll the required skill check against the item's Crafting DC. Any check total exceeding the DC is added as progress.
- Results:
    - Critical Success: Add 10 progress.
    - Success: Add progress equal to the difference between your roll and the DC.
    - Failure: No progress.
    - Critical Failure: Suffer a setback, losing 5 progress.
- The item is completed once all progress tracks are filled.
