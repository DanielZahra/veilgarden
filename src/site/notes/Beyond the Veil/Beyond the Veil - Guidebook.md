# **Introduction**

## What is Beyond the Veil?

Beyond the Veil (Veil) is a high-fantasy tabletop RPG with tactical action based combat, simultaneous turns and flexible horizontal progression that allows anyone to build the character of their dreams with little to no restrictions. There are no classic ability scores or fiddly modifiers. Instead, challenges have a Difficulty Check (DC) and most outcomes use **four degrees of success**. Rolls are rarely modified with additional dice and small bonuses and penalties. Players shape their characters through Masteries, Skills and Equipment, choosing from tactical and narrative options that run on a **3-action economy with 1 Reaction**. Magic and other powers aren’t a spell list, they are expressed as abilities within the masteries or skills that anyone can learn if they meet the prerequisites. Veil is not a game spending time on minute details, or maximizing your attributes and stats. You will find that some features are built so you spend more time roleplaying and enjoying the game rather than tracking modifiers on a character sheet.

# Creator’s Words

Veil lets you build characters without chasing small modifiers. As you gain more experience and grow in power, you don’t inflate numbers, you unlock more options to solve problems. Pick tools that help you achieve your goals rather than just make die and modifiers bigger. In the world of Veil, you will often find descriptions that use the words, magic, arcane or mana. Veil is designed for a world far removed from our own real world where all creatures can control a semblance of mana or magic and the supernatural is a part of the world. The people that live in these worlds use mana as a second nature as they develop the skill to control it, just as they would develop their muscles and other everyday skills.

# Main Resolution Mechanic

You’ll often make a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Veil Roll\|Veil Roll]]: roll a d20 vs a Difficulty (DC). Every roll matters. Most outcomes use **four degrees of success**. **Mastery** gates power and tiers: Adept, Veteran and Master. **In Veil whenever you have to round numbers, you ‘Always Round Down’. When rolling in Veil, the roll measures your degree of success or failure, even simply when you are attacking with a weapon, the roll measures how successful your damage is rather than measuring whether you hit or miss, then rolling for damage separately. 
## 4 Degrees of Success and Failure**

When making a Veil Roll against a DC, if you roll 10 or higher than the DC, it is considered a critical success. Conversely if you roll 10 or lower than the DC, it is considered a critical failure. When you roll a 20, on the Veil Roll, your ‘degree of success is increased by one’, conversely if you roll a 1, your degree of success is reduced by 1. The natural 20/1 step applies to the **d20** after considering any modifiers then compare the final total to the check goal to determine degree, applying the step up or down. To achieve more success and avoid failure the party needs to help each other. With Veil having little modifiers, every small bonus counts.
## **Boons and Banes**

Veil rolls can be mainly modified with a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon/Boon\|Boon]] and/or a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane/Bane\|Bane]]. Boons are bonuses and give you the edge in most situations while Banes are harmful effects, penalties or debilitating situations. Boons and Banes can be minor or major. A **minor** Boon or Bane is a + or - 2 modifier added to the veil roll. While a **major **Boon or Bane is a + or - 4 to the veil roll. Boons and Banes are applied to specific rolls. The feature, condition, ability, or circumstance that provides the boon or bane will specify when it applies.
### Boon and Bane stacking

When making a veil roll, there are specific rules that apply when several boons and banes are present.
- A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon/Boon\|Boon]] or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane/Bane\|Bane]]  adds or subtracts 2.
- A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon/Major Boon\|Major Boon]] or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Bane/Major Bane\|Major Bane]] adds or subtracts 4.
- Regardless of many boons or banes you have, the maximum added or subtracted for a roll is +4 or - 4.
- Minor and major boon or banes are additive with each other on the same roll;
    - E.g. If you have a major boon and a minor bane, your roll is made with a +2.
    - E.g.. if you have 3 minor banes and a minor boon, your roll is still made with a -4.
### **Open Rolls**
Veil’s dice rolling is usually done in the open for the whole table to see. The only time where a roll is done in secret, it is indicated by a **Secret trait**.

# **Heroes in Veil**
When building your character you start by choosing their origins. You choose a species/sub-species and 2 origins and pick 4 knowledge topics or you can create your own custom origin . Every character starts with at least one known language. As you put together your origin choices, you build yourself your character’s background which can also be polished with a written backstory if you desire.
## [[Species\|Species]]

Choose a **species**. Species primarily shape **size, silhouette, and flavor**. Your species has no bearing on what you can do within the game.
## [[Origins\|Origins]]

Origin represents where you have spent a good part of your life, did you live in a fortified city, or lived in a small village? Did you get into education, profession or job prior to starting the adventure? Origins give you your starting knowledge and some minor unlocks towards progression. Regardless of the origin you start with 10 [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Wealth\|Wealth]] , a set of clothing and a [[Backpack\|Backpack]]
## **Unique Aspect & Backstory**

Your character’s One Aspect (their *unique*) is a special feature invented by you, the player, which sets your character apart from every other person in the world. It is a unique and special trait to your player, and markedly unusual. The intent is that it provides a special flavor to the campaign and can assist the Director in determining how your character can interact with characters and story in the campaign. Your character’s *uniqueness* should not provide general practical value in combat. That is not the intent. The intent is to open up story arcs and fun roleplaying opportunities.
## Character Progression
As you gain power in the worlds of Veil, you grow your repertoire of abilities and skills while unlock new ways to improve what you already know. Numbers do not automatically go up. Your character does learn better and stronger abilities, or find strong magical gear as they adventure through the tiers of play. However this is not shown directly in increasing numbers. [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]], Weapon Damage and Ability damage. This applies to both characters and monsters.
## Tiers of Play
The tiers of play are divided into three. Adept, Veteran and Master. These tiers demonstrate your experience, renown and training as a party.

- **Adept**: Adept ranges from the fresh but competent adventurer that is a cut above the rest of their peers. When the town militia or guard fail to combat a threat, Adepts are called in to handle the problem.
- **Veteran**: As a Veteran, you have had your fair share of adventures, successes and failures. These results are shown in your magical gear, allies and renown you have. When a country leader or king calls on a brave group of adventurers, Veterans will often respond to the call and take on the challenge.
- **Master**: When you survive life as a veteran and keep accumulating power, wealth, magical gear and artifacts, and experience, your story becomes legendary. You become the Master. As a master, there are very few people in the world that match your ability and skill. Masters deal with the largest of threats that would be an impossible challenge for common mortals.

# Experience Points (XP)

**When you start your adventure you have an amount of XP, which your character would have gained throughout their life. The default starting value is 28 XP (28 Mastery XP and 28 Skill XP) , but you and/or the director can decide to go higher or lower depending on their style and game type.**

XP is divided into two values, Mastery XP and Skill XP, whenever you gain XP, both Mastery and Skill XP gain the value. However they can be both spent separately. This is intended to allow you to develop a competent character in different directions. 

As you adventure, find secrets, survive challenges, fight dangerous creatures or negotiate with all sorts of sentient beings, You gain XP.

- A trivial challenge no significant danger or risk yields no XP
- A challenge with some risks yields 1 XP
- A very risky challenge which has significant danger yields 2 XP
- Challenges that push you to the limits yield 3 or 4 XP

During character creation or downtime, you can spend Mastery XP to buy your Masteries (Disciplines and Equipment), Abilities and Signature Abilities. And you can spend Skill XP to buy Skill Proficiencies and Skill Features.

| Item | XP Cost |
| --- | --- |
| Discipline or Equipment Mastery | 4 |
| Ability or Signature Ability Upgrade | 2 |
| Skill Proficiency or Skill Feature | 4 |

You can spend XP as a point buy system. Your available options are based on the current tier of play you’re in. That is that you can only buy what is available in your tier (Adept, Veteran or Master).

# Masteries and Skills

**Masteries and Skills** reflect the disciplines, equipment, and skills you develop. These are the core building blocks that define what you can do across combat, social interaction, exploration, and everything in between. As you gain experience and power, your capacity to learn and refine these grows.

### Masteries and Skill Buying (Unlock)

When you spend XP to learn a Mastery (such as Stormweaver or Swords), you ‘unlock’ the mastery and you can then spend more XP into the mastery itself to learn abilities or upgrade them into signature abilities. When you move into a new **Tier of Play, **you can upgrade your already known masteries to the next tier (which gives you access to more options). To unlock higher tiers of a mastery you need to unlock the previous tier beforehand. (eg. You cannot unlock Master in Swords, unless you were already a Veteran in Swords).

When you spend XP to learn a Skill Proficiency, your Veil roll for said skill (Such as Subterfuge) is made with advantage (roll 2d20 and take the highest). Unlocking a skill proficiency also allows you to buy skill features that belong to your chosen skill proficiency.  Unlike masteries, once you have a skill proficiency, when you move into the next tier of play, you can simply buy skill features from the next tier without needing to upgrade.

**You cannot spend XP in Masteries or Skills that you have not unlocked, or are proficient in.**

### Mastery & Skill Limits

You have mortal limits and when choosing discipline and equipment masteries and skill proficiencies. These limits grow as you move through the tiers of play. This means that when you hit your limit, you cannot spend more XP to learn more Masteries or Skill Proficiencies. This does not stop you from learning from abilities or skill features.

| Tier | Mastery Limit | Skill Proficiency Limit |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Adept | 4 | 3 |
| Veteran | 5 | 4 |
| Master | 6 | 5 |

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery/Mastery\|Mastery]] 

A mastery encapsulates your training, education, practice or innate capabilities at performing a specific set of abilities, control of function with particular equipment.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery/Discipline Mastery\|Discipline Mastery]]

**Discipline Mastery** is a trained school of techniques, martial, magical, or mixed (e.g., Pyromancy, Berserker, Vanguard). Your rank (Adept, Veteran or Master) in a Discipline gates which abilities and passives you can learn. Higher ranks unlock new actions, features and abilities. You spend XP to learn abilities within the Disciplines you possess or to rank up in the Discipline itself. Each **Discipline Mastery** has a passive or active ability that is unlocked and improved as you invest more XP into the Discipline.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery/Equipment Mastery\|Equipment Mastery]] 

**Equipment Mastery** defines your abilities and proficiencies with weapons, armor and other specialized equipment. As you invest your XP into Equipment masteries, you can learn more abilities and gain passive traits and effects for the chosen piece of equipment. Abilities gained from Equipment Mastery, generally need the equipment to be activated and used.
#### Equipment Families

Some of the Equipment masteries are tied to a family of items rather than a very specific item type. For example, the Sword family of items includes short swords, long swords and great swords. While these weapons have different damage, handling and traits, they share the same equipment mastery tree.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery/Companion Mastery\|Companion Mastery]] 

Companion Mastery typically involves you controlling another creature as a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Companion\|Companion]] . As you invest XP into this mastery, your companion gets stronger and gains more traits and you both learn more abilities to act in tandem.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery/Core Mastery\|Core Mastery]] 

Core mastery is focused on change, adapting or breaking the mold for how you act. A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Mastery/Core Mastery\|Core Mastery]] has a single significant defining feature but has a restricted amount of abilities. Core masteries typically are better suited to support other masteries.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Signature/Signature\|Signature]] Abilities

Starting at Veteran you can start spending XP to make an ability a signature ability, you can enhance it with new effects or change its effects to do something new during its use. When using a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Signature/Signature\|Signature]] ability you can choose only a **single** signature effect, and you can also choose not to add any effect to your ability.

## Skill Proficiency

When unlocking a skill proficiency, your Veil Rolls for said skill check roll 2d20s and you take the highest roll (This is called Advantage). For skills which you are not proficient in, you roll a single d20.

## Skill Features

As you gain more skill features by spending XP,  you open new possibilities, learn skill abilities and also gain passive benefits related to your proficient skills.

## Skill Checks

**Skill Checks** in Veil are only rolled when there is a consequence or risk of failing. When dealing with the environment or making a skill check against the static ‘world at large’ most checks fall within the following. These skill checks do not change with the party, they are persistent and each individual deals with them in their own way. Skill checks made against other creatures have their DC based on the statistics of the creature based on its abilities.

- DC 4 Simple
- DC 8 Standard
- DC 12 Tricky
- DC 16 Difficult
- DC 20 Extreme
- Impossible (no rolling)

### Abilities as Skill Checks

Some abilities might be used in a pinch instead of a skill check, when this happens the Director can decide to give an automatic success or the check is made as if it has a successful help action. This might bypass some checks too.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Group Skill Check\|Group Skill Check]] 

When an event calls for a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Group Skill Check\|Group Skill Check]], you (the party) all roll against 1 or more DCs, and each success grants ‘points’ towards a success. Each failure grants a failure point. At the end of the skill challenge, if the number of successes is equal or higher than failure, you succeed.

| **Degree**       | **Effect**           |
| ---------------- | -------------------- |
| Critical Success | You gain 2 successes |
| Success          | You gain 1 success   |
| Failure          | You gain a failure   |
| Critical Failure | You gain 2 failures  |
### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Skirmish\|Skirmish]] 

A skirmish is a quick combat [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Group Skill Check\|Group Skill Check]] that does not go into the nitty gritty of encounter mode and serves to quickly resolve encounters. A skirmish, is a group skill challenge where you are guaranteed to win and the rolls are made to measure your performance.

A Skirmish has a set DC, typically particular approaches are better suited than others depending on the situation and their DC is lower. This is handled by the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Director\|Director]] and often not revealed.

Example:

- A group of bandits are aware of the you. You can charge in [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Athletics\|Athletics]] or surprise them [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Subterfuge\|Subterfuge]].
- A group of assassins surprise you, you roll [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Perception\|Perception]] on the chance spotting them, or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Agility\|Agility]] to react quickly.
- A pack of wolves have surrounded the you, you can use [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Smarts\|Smarts]] to create a strategy on the fly, or growl back to scare them into breaking formation using [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Skills/Influence\|Influence]].

On a success the party defeats their opponents easily, on a failure, the party defeats the opponents easily but the [[luck\|luck]] die is rolled. If you roll a critical failure (personal roll), you also gain a Reaper’s Mark representing a blunder on your side or the opponents getting a lucky shot on you.

|**Degree**|**Effect**|
|---|---|
|Critical Success|You gain 2 successes|
|Success|You gain 1 success|
|Failure|You gain a failure|
|Critical Failure|You gain 2 failures, and you personally gain 1 Reaper’s Mark.|

### Secret Checks

Skill checks, abilities or anything with the Secret Traits signifies that the Director makes the Veil Roll without revealing the result, they only narrate the outcome.

### Skill Challenges

Skill challenges are a special set of group skills checks that are run as a group. These challenges are used in cases where you have to complete a set number of obstacles, movement traps, without going into encounter mode. Most of the skill challenges are run in Exploration Mode.

# [[Campcraft\|Campcraft]]

When you and your party take rest or a longer vacation from your adventures you can interact with **Campcraft**. This is where you can unlock, buy, and modify the vast majority of adventuring gear, tools, kits, consumables and also spend your wealth. Campcraft allows the logistics of the adventure to be managed in one place.

### **Wealth**

**Wealth** represents the party’s aggregated valuables—coins, gems, trophies—abstracted into a single unit. You spend Wealth to unlock items via **Campcraft**, purchase gear, or sway NPCs. Routine living costs are hand-waved; **the Director calls for Wealth** only for exceptional expenses (luxury lodging, feasts, bribes).

*Wealth is abstract. One unit of Wealth might represent a bag of 10 gold pieces, a bundle of valuable furs, or a piece of jewelry. You as a party are limited by how much wealth you can carry.*

**The Director is responsible for deciding if something costs wealth or not.**

### Consumables

When you use Wealth to purchase consumables recipes, every time you rest and use downtime you completely refill the consumable stack. Special downtime actions allow you to temporarily increase your stocks. (e.g. When you purchase the Healing Potion recipe, after every rest you have 4 Healing Potions to use. You can decide who is holding them, or simply keep them in the Backpack.)

Different consumables have different stacks and through Campcraft and downtime actions you can add, improve and modify certain aspects of each consumable item.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Loadout\|Loadout]]

You start Veil with 6 Loadout spaces. Loadout are the items, weapons, or gear you have quick access to. Weapons and Shields have to be in your Loadout to be used, held items count against your Loadout. Loadout can be increased in several ways. Loadout is mainly important for when the game is using** Encounter Mode **where every second counts.

Secondary Weapons, Potions, Wands, Charms/Trinkets, Bombs, Etc. should be in your Loadout if you want to easily use them.

Taking an item from your Backpack requires you to spend 3 actions on a  [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Backpack interaction\|Backpack Interaction]], to fetch the item and use it. (if the item requires more than one action to use, you simply hold it).

Items with a stack number (such as potion) take 1 Loadout slot per use/item.  (e.g. Carrying 2 Stamina Potions, means you need 2 Loadout Slots).

### [[Backpack\|Backpack]]

The Backpack is considered to be the party’s shared inventory. This holds all your supplies, tools, camping gear, backup equipment, magical gear and everything else that you would want to fit. You, the players do not have to keep track of who has what all the time. Items that have stacks, only count for 1 Backpack space. You start Veil with 20 Backpack Slots **shared** between the party

Separately, you also have space for your accumulating loot measured in Wealth. Wealth has its own slots which start at 20. This measures the amount of treasure, loot, and money the party can realistically carry as they move along. Don’t hoard wealth, spend it.

As you adventure, solve problems, make friends and gain more wealth, you can upgrade your Backpack to carry more, increasing the Backpack Slots and total amount of Wealth you can carry.

**When your Backpack Slots or Total amount of Wealth carried limits are reached, you cannot loot or carry more items.**

### Cargo

Cargo is additional storage that you have in your camp, this represents the items that are not carried with the party all the time. [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cargo\|Cargo]] can be used and accessed during [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Rest\|Rest]].

### Gear Slots ([[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Worn\|Worn]] Items)

 [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Worn\|Worn]] items, gear or weapons allowed them to used without being held and they are on your person.  They might require [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Interact\|Interact]] action to use or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Strike\|Strike]] to be used. [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Worn\|Worn]] [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Weapons/Weapons\|Weapons]]  typically, require a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Loadout\|Loadout]] slot. [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Worn\|Worn]] items include the following categories; [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Boots\|Boots]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Eyewear\|Eyewear]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Headwear\|Headwear]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Cape\|Cape]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Gloves\|Gloves]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Chestpiece\|Chestpiece]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Worn/Belt\|Belt]].
## Equipment

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Weapons/Weapons\|Weapons]]

**Weapons** list a fixed **Damage**, **Basic Traits** which describe how the basic parts of the weapon work and its properties , and **Custom Traits** (chosen at Adept/Veteran/Master, can be swapped in downtime via Crafting). Damage doesn’t scale with levels. Finally each weapon mastery unlocks a Modification** at Veteran.**

### Weapon Variety

Weapons are grouped by **Family** (e.g., *Swords*) and by **Type** (e.g., *Short Sword, Long Sword*). **Equipment Mastery** keys off **Type**; runes and some abilities can target **Family**. Types within a Family share core identity (typical damage types, common autos, compatible crit effect), while differing on hands, reach, and trait mix. Some weapons can belong to multiple families allowing them to use abilities from both masteries.

### **Protection**

You start with standard protective clothing that sets your **Defense to 12.** Its look (leather, chain, etc.) is flavor. You can buy and invest in **Armor, Wards or Vestment Equipment Mastery which** let you modify and upgrade this armor into specific protective clothing and gives specialized traits.

## Magical Items

Magic gear is the cornerstone of any adventurer, as the party travels the world, defeating dungeons, monsters and completing quests they might be awarded magical gear that makes them stronger. Magical gear comes in different types and flavors. Different items have different powers, uses and expirations.

### Shards

Shards are unstable trinkets, scrolls or other items with magic infused in them. When you utilize a shard to unleash magic within, you roll a 1d4. On a 1, the shard is permanently destroyed after it unleashes its magic one last time.

### Exalted

Exalted items possess strong mana and magic that permits them to significantly provide power to a soulbound creature.

### Runes

Unlike other magical items runes are magic designed to enhance other objects. Adventurers are mainly looking for runes that enhance their weapons and armor. Runes can be added to your gear and also removed or replaced during downtime.  Often runes can only be used on specific types of gear to match their intent. (eg. A rune made for Slashing weapons cannot be used on a Bow).

### Artifacts

Artifacts are the pinnacle of magical power infused into items. Most artifacts are unique and some consider them to bend reality. An artifact can potentially change your life completely if you happen to own one.

Artifacts are completely bound to the soulbinder’s mana and power. As the soulbinder grows in power the artifacts 'grow in power with them’. As you move between the tiers of play. The artifact can achieve a higher degree of power.

### **Soulbinding**

Using magical gear comes at a cost. You cannot use as much magic without putting your life at risk. When using magical gear you need to spend time performing a Soulbinding to the magic items. This allows you to channel mana and activate the effects of the items.

**You can Soulbind 6 magical items.**

# Knowledge

When building your character, most choices you make give you knowledge domains or specific knowledge.

Knowledge is not a skill, it is simply the sum of what topics you are versed about. When you have knowledge about a particular domain you can make a knowledge check to see what you know. The knowledge topics do not cover everything, as such it is important to be able to make connections and allow flexibility when making these checks.

- **Basic Knowledge**
    - Basic knowledge is what people in a specific area, time, etc would know. This never needs a roll. And most characters are expected to know this as long as they are not extremely far away from home and in a somewhat alien place or different place of existence with different metaphysical laws.
- **Domain Knowledge**
    - Domain knowledge is the knowledge of a specific topic or subject that the character would know about. For example: undead is a domain topic so if a character has this Domain knowledge they would know a lot about undead.
    - Some simple knowledge can be given automatically but usually you will need to do a knowledge check to see what you know about the given topic.
- **Specialized Knowledge**
    - Specialized knowledge is always part of a larger domain. But about a specific subset. (eg. Wolves being part of Mammals. When you make a **Specialized **Knowledge check, you roll with** Advantage**.
    - Specialized Knowledge is often harder to obtain as opposed to regular domain knowledge.

The Director is the judge of what knowledge belongs in which category based on the setting or subject.

## **Knowledge Checks**

**Knowledge Checks,** the Director may roll these in secret. Knowledge checks are only made when the knowledge needed aren’t simple things within a domain. Knowledge checks can only be made **ONCE** per character per topic.

Knowledge checks are meant to be generous, when you have knowledge about a domain that is relevant for the topic, it is assumed that you can roll.

When you have multiple domains that can be used for the check, you gain a Minor Boon for each relevant topic. Such as If you have both mammal lore and mountain lore, and you’re trying to figure out if you know which mammals live in a range of mountains, this roll gets a Boon for possessing both topics.

| **Degree**       | **Effect**                                                |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Critical Success | You know **everything** there is to know about the topic. |
| Success          | You know **plenty** about this topic.                     |
| Failure          | You know** little** about this topic.                     |
| Critical Failure | You know **nothing** about this topic.                    |

### Knowledge Checks about Creatures or Monsters

When making the above Knowledge Check about Monsters the check is made about the general creature and monster type rather than a specific creature.

| **Degree** | **Effect** |
| --- | --- |
| Critical Success | You know the creature’s abilities (all of them), behaviour, movement types and habitat and general lore about them.<br><br>Any banes or boons the creature might have. |
| Success | You know the creature’s main ability, behaviour, movement types and habitat and general lore about them. |
| Failure | You know general lore about the creature. |

## **Learning and gaining Knowledge**

Throughout your career and adventures you will be exposed to many fascinating things, dangers, treasure and also find yourself learning a lot of new things. A wise one once said that knowledge is power, thus gaining knowledge can be very beneficial for you and yours.

### Knowledge Capacity

Basic knowledge and languages are not part of the knowledge capacity. For each [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Knowledge\|Knowledge]] domain or specialized topic you know you add 1 knowledge capacity. This capacity is used as a Knowledge DC when you roll the [[Learning\|Learning]] Checks. When you want to learn new things you need to do three things. Acquire material to study, study and repeat until you succeed.

### Study Material

When you want to study a particular domain, or rare information, you need to acquire the study material. This could be a book, a magical item or codex, studying under a tutor or master etc. The Director is responsible for deciding if the provided conditions allow for learning.

Learning (Downtime). Spend downtime studying appropriate material. You make a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Skill Check/Smarts Skill Check\|Smarts Skill Check]] + Learning bonuses; succeed if the total exceeds your current knowledge DC. On failure, you gain Deep Study on your next attempt for this Domain (stacking for the same Domain).

The more you know the harder it becomes to learn more. When you make a Learning roll, you roll [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Skill Check/Smarts Skill Check\|Smarts Skill Check]] + any bonuses to learning. You have to match or beat your current DC. Several features will increase your knowledge capacity and also add bonuses to your learning.

When you fail a Learning Roll, the next learning roll about the same Domain is made with a Bonus. This Bonus stacks on itself to ensure that eventually you succeed.

Most abilities in Veil can be used once and they need a [[Take a Break\|Take a Break]] to be used again. Some Rituals and specific abilities might have longer cooldowns or limited to once per day. The default cooldown is referred to as cooldown.

Most of the consumable items run out. Consumables that you have built through your [[Campcraft\|Campcraft]] can be resupplied with the rest. Consumables that are bought or found in the world do not replenish once used.

## Death

### Reaper’s Marks

Reaper’s Marks show how close a hero is to dying. They represent the wounds, injuries, stress and trauma a party of heroes suffer as they adventure, fight and suffer dangers of the world.

Each Hero starts with a maximum of 6 Reaper’s Marks. Once a hero reaches 6 Reaper’s Marks, they die permanently.

- When you reach 0 [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]], you immediately gain a Reaper’s Mark. 
	- This signifies that you’re at your limits and any further damage you take gives you another [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Reaper’s Mark\|Reaper’s Mark]].
- If you take damage equal to double your Maximum [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]], you die outright. (this is considered a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Fatality\|Fatality]])
- If you take damage Equal to your maximum [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]] but lower than 2x your maximum [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]], you go [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Content/Conditions/Unconscious\|Unconscious]] (this is considered a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Colossal Blow\|Colossal Blow]])
- Reaper’s Marks are reduced by two with every [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Rest\|Rest]]
    - Skills, Consumables and Abilities might allow you to recover more quickly.
    - This does mean that resting in a dungeon doesn’t typically automatically clear all marks.

### Hazards

- While out of encounter mode, you can easily stay at full endurance. The party has to expect that Traps are built to hurt, main and ideally kill intruders. So traps likely deal direct Reaper’s Marks instead of [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]].
    - Some more complicated Traps or Hazard might deal normal damage but these are executed in [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]].

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]] is used for when every round, turn and decision needs to be measured as each action has impact. This mode is used mainly for combat, complex traps. [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]] is measured in rounds and turns. A turn is when a single creature uses its turn. When all creatures have taken their turn, a round is completed. The order in which each creature acts is handled by [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative\|Initiative]].

### **Action Economy**

### Actions & Reactions

As you start your turn, you gain 3 actions and a single reaction.

[[Universal Actions\|Universal Actions]]: Universal actions do not have a cooldown and can be used repeatedly, provided you meet certain requirements. You have access to call basic actions.

[[Mastery Actions\|Mastery Actions]]: Some actions need to be unlocked by specific Skills or Masteries.

### **Attacking**

All attacks (any action or ability with an Attack trait are made by using the Veil Roll and adding your Prowess boons or banes.

### Attacking and Traits

When you make an attack or strike with a specific damage type, the attack automatically gains the corresponding trait. E.g., if you make an axe attack with added fire damage, your axe attack will also have the ‘Fire’ Trait, alongside the Slashing trait.

### Attack Types

- **Attack** - When an action or ability calls for an attack, you make a Veil Roll and add any boon or bane to your prowess against the target’s **Defense**.
    - Example: You are shooting a fire bolt towards a wolf, which has Defense of 8. You make a Veil Roll (Prowess) and roll a 9, this means your attack was successful and you resolve the attack accordingly.
- **Strike** - when an action or ability calls for a **strike**, it is an attack that utilizes your held weapon or unarmed combat, this means all traits and weapon properties are applied to the strike. You make a Veil Roll and add any modifiers to your prowess against the target’s Defense.
    - Example: You are striking a wolf, which has Defense of 8. You make a Veil Roll and roll a 9, this means your Strike was successful and you resolve accordingly.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power Attack\|Power Attack]] a power attack is an attack that deals half the damage on a failure or failure. These attacks are usually parts of more abilities or features. 
- [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Strike Action/Power Strike\|Power Strike]] **Power Strikes, are strikes that deal half damage on failure.
- **Overpower** - When [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Overpower/Overpower\|Overpower]] is present the attack targets the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Defense\|Defense]] normally, however the ability or feature will also target a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Fortitude\|Fortitude]], [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Reflex\|Reflex]] or [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Will\|Will]]. When this occurs, the result of the roll made for the attack or strike is used for both checks. An [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Overpower/Overpower\|Overpower]] can only be triggered if **any damage** is dealt to [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Vitality\|Vitality]] of the target. If the whole damage is negated by a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Barrier/Barrier\|Barrier]], a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Boon/Boon\|Boon]] or [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Mastery Actions/Shield Block\|Shield Block]] or other damage reductions, [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Overpower/Overpower\|Overpower]] is not applied.
- **Prowess rolls that target Fortitude, Reflex or Will** - some abilities are not meant to target a creature’s Defenses directly. One such attack is the [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Shove\|Shove]] action. In case of shove, it rolls directly against the target’s Fortitude, so you make a Veil Roll modified by your Prowess against the target creature’s Fortitude.
    - Example: You are trying to [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Shove\|Shove]] a wolf backwards, the wolf has a Fortitude of 10. You make a Veil Roll and add your Prowess modifiers. Let’s say you roll a 9 and you have a bonus of 2, leading to a total of 11. Your Shove succeeds.
- **Unarmed Attacks - **Unarmed attacks have some specific conditions. Unarmed attack can be done with any part of the body such as head butting, punching, kicking and using your tail. Without being at least an Adept in unarmed combat, you deal 1 Crushing Damage and you cannot Engage opponents without training.

### **Abilities**

Abilities take many shapes or forms but combined together they form the capabilities of a character within many scenarios. Abilities can be acquired  from different sources but the main source of acquisition are by spending ability points and skill points.

### Basic Defenses

When a [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Prowess\|Prowess]] roll targets a one of the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Basic Defenses/Basic Defenses\|Basic Defenses]] (one of the 3), the damage being applied and the degree of success is applied as below:

| Degree           | Effect        |
| ---------------- | ------------- |
| Critical Success | Double Damage |
| Success          | Full Damage   |
| Failure          | Half Damage   |
| Critical Failure | No Effect     |
### **Dealing Damage**

Most of the damage dealt in Veil is listed on the weapons and abilities. Most abilities, actions, or weapons define exactly how much damage they can deal and what type of damage they deal too. Damage is listed as a flat number, with variation based on how well you roll. Each Damage also includes a type.

### Calculating Damage (order)

When an attack or ability resolves, determine the damage path based on the roll result. Calculate the final value for **each damage type individually** using the corresponding steps below:

- Normal Damage
    1. Base: Apply the listed Damage value (e.g., 6)
    2. Modifiers: Add any [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/additional damage\|additional damage]] effects.
    3. Banes and Boons: Calculate banes or boons of the target against the total value for each damage type individually.
- Half Damage
    1. Base: Apply the listed Damage value (e.g., 6)
    2. Modifiers: Add any [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/additional damage\|additional damage]] effects.
    3. Halve: Half each Damage Type individually.
    4. Banes and Boons: Calculate banes or boons of the target against the total value for each damage type individually.
- Critical Damage Calculation
    1. Base: Apply the listed Damage value (e.g., 6)
    2. Multiply: [[Base Damage \|Base Damage ]]by 2
    3. Modifiers: Add any [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/additional damage\|additional damage]] effects.
    4. Critical Effect: Apply any additional [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Critical Effect\|Critical Effect]]
    5. Banes and Boons: Calculate banes or boons of the target against the total value for each damage type individually.

### Damage Immunity

When you have a damage immunity against a damage type, you cannot take any damage from said type of damage.

### Boon against damage

When you have a boon to a type of damage, each time you take that type of damage, you reduce the amount of damage taken by 2 for a minor boon and 4 for a major boon. This can bring the damage down to 0.

It's possible to have boon to all damage types. When an effect deals damage of multiple types and you have boon to all damage, apply the boon to each type of damage separately. If an attack would deal 7 slashing damage and 4 fire damage, a major boon to all damage would reduce the slashing damage to 3 and negate the fire damage entirely.

### Bane against Damage

If you have a bane to a certain type of damage or damage from a certain source, that type of damage is extra effective against you. Whenever you take that type of damage, increase the damage you take by 2 for a minor bane and +4 for a major bane. For instance, if you are dealt 4 fire damage and have major bane against fire, you take 8 (4+4) fire damage.

### Lethal and Non-Lethal Damage

Normally when dealing damage, the damage dealt is always lethal and can kill. You can declare a non lethal attack or ability, when dealing damage this way, your degree of success is reduced by one.

Some abilities or weapons have the Non-Lethal Trait, which allows them to deal normal damage but declare that it is non-lethal.

### Critical Damage and Critical Effect

When critical damage is dealt you simply double the damage dealt with a strike or ability. Additional, Imbue or Rider damage that applies additionally to the main effect is not doubled unless specifically indicated.

When an ability, trait or other effect is applied on a critical success, critical effect or critical damage. These effects are applied after the critical damage is calculated. Any exception to this rule has specific wording that indicates it.

## **Area of Effect**

Abilities or effects that use an area of effect are always defined in an area by distance. The following are the area types used in Veil.

When playing on a grid, Veil does not use circles but only squares. A burst of 5x5 on a grid means 3 meters, squares, or inches away from 1 point, in any direction.

### Burst

A burst is always measured as square, for example a 5x5 meter burst. When using a burst area of effect at range, your range is measured from the middle of the burst.

### Emanation

An emanation is similar to a burst, however an emanation’s source of origin is always an entity or a creature.

### Line

Line areas of effect are defined as length by width and the origin of the user of the ability unless indicated otherwise. For example a 10x2 line is 10 long, 2 wide.

### [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cone/Cone\|Cone]]

Cone areas of effect are defined only by their length. When using a grid, a cone starts with 1 square adjacent to the entity using the cone and  increases the width by 2 for each meter in length.

## **Tactical**

### Engaging

When you are in an **adjacent** space with a creature, you are considered to be **Engaged** with the creature. If an ally of yours also engages with the same creature, the creature gains the Exposed condition making it easier to land successful blows.

You can only engage a creature if you meet certain conditions:

- You must be in an adjacent space.
- The opposing creature must be able to see you (this can vary based on the creature’s senses).
- You must not be unconscious or incapacitated.
- You must be wielding a weapon with the melee/reach trait.
    - For the purposes of Engage (Reach weapons do not engage a creature unless you’re in an adjacent space)
    - If you are trained in Unarmed combat, your body is considered to be a melee weapon.

### Cover

When you’re positioned behind something that can block blows, blunt blasts, you can use the [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Take Cover\|Take Cover]] action to gain the benefits of [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Cover/Cover\|Cover]].

**When you’re targeted and you have cover**, roll **1d6** after the attack or prowess check is rolled.  On a success, **shift the outcome one step in your favor**:

- Attacks: Critical to a Successful, Successful to a Failure, Failure to Critical Failure

Cover is relative to each attacker. You might have cover against one foe but not another. Cover applies only if the path from attacker to target is **partially blocked**; if a barrier fully blocks the path, you lack **line of effect** and can’t be targeted through it.

When you use the [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Take Cover\|Take Cover]] action, you gain the benefits of the cover, but the cover does not obscurity you from attacking your foes or supporting your allies.

The [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Director\|Director]] usually rules this at a glance. If you need precision, draw a line from the **center of your space** to the **center of the target’s space**:

- The line crossing **terrain/objects,** **standard** cover (or **heavy** if the obstruction is extreme or the target has Taken Cover).
- The line crossing a **creature,** **light** cover. For area effects, draw from the effect’s **point of origin** to the target’s center.

If a creature standing between you and your target is one or more sizes larger than both of you, it provides standard (not light) cover. Likewise, a creature might be too large to benefit from small terrain (e.g., a Huge beast behind a small trunk of a tree).

Situational rulings can reduce or negate cover. Examples: firing out of an arrow slit next to you suffers no penalty, while incoming shots against you might grant you heavy cover; and the Director can measure from an edge or corner of your space instead of the center when appropriate.

- **Light cover** (low wall, ally, foliage): succeeds on **6** (≈16.7%)
- **Standard cover** (half wall, doorframe): succeeds on **5-6** (≈33%)
- **Heavy cover** (arrow slit, narrow murder hole): succeeds on **4-6** (≈50%)
- **Total cover**: can’t be targeted.

# [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative\|Initiative]]

When [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]] starts if your party ‘take’ the initiative your side gets to go first, than the NPCs. Each side gets to take a turn. If more than 2 parties are involved, they all get a turn. Veil using side based, initiative, so typically you get to go first, than the NPCs. NPCs typically go first on an ambush.

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative Action\|Initiative Action]]

When an ability of a feature has the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Initiative Action\|Initiative Action]], it allows it to be used as a reaction just as initiative starts. This represents the quick danger sense of adventurers when faced with adversity.

### Turns and Rounds

When you take your turn as a party, you all go together and it is considered your turn. The order in which you execute any action is up to you. You can move, attack, use abilities in any order which makes sense. When you’re all done, the other side goes. Some effects with the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Combo\|Combo]] trait allow you to take more simultaneous actions during your turn. When all participants that are part of the encounter have taken a turn, it signifies a round end.

---

### Delaying

You may delay, letting the next faction act first and you go after.

---

### Ambush

When one side manages to completely surprise the other side, i.e. all creatures one side are unaware of the danger, the Ambushers get to act first and until they are revealed the other side does not get their turn. An ambush continues until the ambushers make an action that reveals them, or the enemies otherwise become aware of danger.

Example:

If the party are trying to sneak in a very good position to attack a group of unaware bandits, the games goes into [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]] as every action counts. The party can use the first round to sneak and get closer and in a better position. If by the end of the round, the enemies are still unaware of the party’s presence the Ambush continues.

---

### Multi-Faction Battles

In three or more faction conflicts, play proceeds in a different fashion, with the initiating side acting first. Each faction alternates with the next until all have acted. Specifically the multi faction system is activated when the you have allies that join them in the fight.

Example: In a 3-way battle (PCs, Bandits, Guards), the order might be:

1. PC > Bandits > Guards

### **Combat Timer**

In Veil, the ideal flow is combat is meant to be fast decision making and tactics have to be made on the spot. It is intended that each ‘round’ is concluded in under 10 minutes. That is that you are going in the turn, decide what to do and make all rolls within the 10 minute cap. The same is applied to the director when controlling NPCs. Faster combat allows a game session to move at a better pace and everyone plays more often.

When the turn timer is over, any action in play is resolved and pending actions are lost.

# [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] treats time into more malleable chunks rather than looking into exactly what everyone does every second. During [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] the players can choose what their characters are doing as they move around, explore, search, scout, sneak, etc. 

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] is used when the party is performing rituals, exploring dungeons, or doing other activities. In general, most ‘quick’ activities take around 10 minutes, while some others can take hours or days. Available activities are found in [[Exploration Activities\|Exploration Activities]].

---

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] is mainly split into 3 gameplay loops: Passive Activities, Active Activities, and Dungeon Turns. Dungeon turns are segments of time that pass as you explore. The passive actions chosen by you, are what you’re doing as time passes. This includes how fast or carefully you move, if you’re looking for clues, traps, secrets, or enemies lying in wait to ambush you.

The [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] active party is used when you or allies switch from something passive to doing something more ‘active.’ Using actions does not require leaving passive mode, as they are quick. However, using rituals or active exploration activities goes into the active state. When you’re taking an active exploration activities you (and the rest of the party) must declare what you’re doing for the next 10 minutes. You can remain in passive mode if you’re not changing what you’re doing. 

---

[[Exploration Activities\|Exploration Activities]] are the most common activities used when you are travelling both in a dangerous spot or a road well travelled. Activities are split into 3 categories, Movement and Passive which are states in which you declare what you are doing ahead of time. And active is where you declare what you are doing now, usually reacting to the environment or interaction.

- **Movement**: Describe how you are moving, this defines the speed, noise, visibility etc., the party always moves at the rate of the slowest party member unless they want to leave them behind.
- **Passive**: Describe what you are doing while exploring, such as detecting mana, covering tracks, following tracks, keeping an eye out for danger, mapping and navigating etc.
- **Active:** Describe when you actively take action based on a finding, environmental effect or interaction during your exploration.

When you move from exploration to [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]], the exploration activities being performed are considered to be what you are doing.

**During ****[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]]****  you can choose an Exploration Activity which is passive. You as a party can choose a single Movement exploration activity as a group which costs no Exploration Activities, as long as you stay as a group. If a single member wants to use a particular Movement Exploration Activity which is different from the rest of the party, it takes one of their Exploration Activities.

If the party is performing an activity and the game moves into encounter mode, activities can still be resumed, the Director is responsible for measuring how many rounds it takes to perform the activity and how many characters need to devote a number of actions to progress the task.

Take a break is an activity where the party takes a 10 minute break when adventuring to quickly recover their ability or item cooldowns and heal to maximum Endurance.

### **10 Minute Turn (Active Exploration)**

When exploring a corridor, a room, reading glyphs on a wall, you should consider that most things that are active exploration activities take around 10 minutes, unless they have a specific duration or time taken. Any ‘current’ exploration activity (passive) you are using is taken automatically.

---

## Doubling Up

When you and an ally both take the same activity, you both make separate rolls and the highest results counts for the whole party.

## Backtracking

When backtracking to previous location you can decide to simply walk rather than explore to speed things up.

### **Push Your Luck aka ‘Luck’**

Exploration needs stakes. If you have infinite time, you can search every tile, pick every lock perfectly, and rest whenever you want. But in **Veil**, time is not on your side. To track the passage of time and the rising danger of your environment, Veil tests your luck against the world, when Luck is not on your side bad things happen.

### How You Use It

Whenever you are exploring, whether delving into a ruin, investigating a crime scene, or trekking through the wilds, your choices dictate the flow of your Luck. The Luck die starts at d20, and every time it is rolled, it degrades the die size by 1. (d20 > d12 >d10 > d8 > d6 >d4 >d2).

When you roll a natural 1, a complication happens and your Luck die goes back to a d20.

### Spending Time

Whenever you commit to an action that is extremely slow, very complicated, or too methodical, you roll your luck.

- *Examples:* Searching a room for loot, taking 10 to rest, or repair items, carefully picking a lock, critically failing some checks which complicate things or ritually identifying a magic item
- **The Rule of Thumb:** Most actions or activities that take 10 or more minutes to perform** roll a luck die.**

### Recklessness and Failure

Whenever you commit to an action that is very loud, rushed, or risky to save time or possibly critically fail a check, you make your luck roll with **disadvantage.** That is you roll two Luck dice simultaneously and take the worse result.

- *Examples:* Smashing a door down instead of picking the lock, shouting an argument in a quiet dungeon, or sprinting through an unknown hallway.
- *Note:* If the pool is empty and you act recklessly, the Director picks up 1 die and rolls it

### Extreme Risks

In some cases when you perform something extremely risky, such as sleeping/resting inside a dangerous area, creating a massive echo in a cave filled with monsters, etc, your luck dice becomes a d2 and is rolled immediately.

### Tipping Point

When a complication happens, a 1 is rolled on any die size, the saving grace is that you go back to having a d20 luck die.

### Complications

When a 1 is rolled you get a **Complication.**

**What is a Complication?** A Complication is rarely a big fight. It means the situation has shifted against you because you took too long or made too much noise.

- **Resources:** Some of your gear breaks
- **Environment:** A path collapses, fog obscures your vision, or a door jams.
- **Threats:** A patrol spots you, a monster wakes up, or your quarry escapes.

Complications are split into, Trivial, Minor, Major and Extreme and rolled on a 2d10 with the extremities being much rarer. In some cases the Director can choose the appropriate complication based on the situation.

# [[Ritual Tome\|Ritual Tome]]

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Social Mode\|Social Mode]] 

**Social Mode** runs tense negotiations, interrogations, debates, and court deals, battles of _words rather than swords._ There is **no initiative**, **no 3-action economy**, and **no reactions**. Instead, the table plays in **free-flowing exchanges**: you speak when it makes sense, and the Director keeps the spotlight moving so both sides trade meaningful “moves.”

You’ll still use **Veil’s four degrees of success** and push two visible clocks: **Favor** (toward winning what you want) and **Backlash** (toward things going badly). **The majority of checks in Social Mode are Secret checks.**

### **The Four Affinities**

All NPCs will have some affinity that they react better to, and all attempts you make uses one of these **affinities** (the opposition is stronger vs some, weaker vs others):

1. **Deception** - misdirection, lies, trap questions, contradictions.
2. **Intimidation** - aggression, violence, pressure, deadlines, implied force.
3. **Diplomacy** - cunning, street smarts, terms, concessions, strategy, legalese.
4. **Charm** - persuasion, rapport, empathy, reputation, flattery.

Each approach has its **own DC** for the opponent. You can discover this during play .

### **Skills and Knowledge in Social Mode**

During social mode, when you are describing your behavior and actions, the skill roll you make is based on the action described. The following table gives an indication how each skill can be used in social mode. In cases where your knowledge is applicable you can make a knowledge roll rather than a skill check. (This usually has a better impact when you have specialized knowledge about a topic).

During a social encounter, you can only use a skill once. When continuing the conversation you can let other party members take over or change your strategy. If you fail to use another skill, it simply counts as a failure.

### **When to use Social Mode?**

If one roll would settle it, the Director just calls for that check. Social Mode is useful when a group of creatures are in a conversation, this includes the whole party. When the outcome is uncertain, high-stakes, or needs back-and-forth, the Director announces **Social Mode** and reveals:

- The **Favor** clock, and
- The **Backlash** clock.
- The scene **immediately ends** when either clock fills.

**Encounter-limited effects** treat the whole Social Mode as **one encounter/scene**.

### **Favor & Backlash**

Every social skill check you roll advances one of the two clocks:

- **Critical Success:** **+2 Favor**
- **Success:** **+1 Favor**
- **Failure:** **+1 Backlash**
- **Critical Failure:** **+2 Backlash**

### **Help Action and Social Mode**

The [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Help\|Help]] action in Social Mode cannot be used in the same way as Exploration since conversation usually happens in real time. Instead the [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Help\|Help]] action can be invoked by specific features or abilities.

### **Close the Veil**

When Social Mode is in play the Director will usually ‘Close the Veil’, this usually entails that player chatter should be down to a minimum and cross talking (meta-discussions) should not be a thing unless a skill check is being made or an ability is used.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Downtime Mode\|Downtime Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Downtime Mode\|Downtime Mode]] is where you can rest and recover for you next leg in the journey. Every day when the you sets up camp or sleeps in an inn, you have some downtime.

A [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Downtime Activity\|Downtime Activity]] can be performed during said downtime. Downtime is measured in 1 single period of rest or extended to days, weeks, months or years. (This is decided by the Director). Downtime activities can be expanded and are deeply linked to Campcraft unlocks.

[[Take a Break\|Take a Break]]

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Rest/Rest\|Rest]]

You can only rest **once** per day (24 hours). Resting takes a **8 hours** for you to set up camp, secure the perimeter, eat, drink, sleep and prepare for the next day. This time also assumes and includes you performing downtime activities. If you decide to rest multiple times in a row to recover further, the above rule of one rest per day still applies so it takes 2 days to accumulate 2 rests. Everything that doesn’t get recovered with a Take a Break Recovers with a Rest.

There are two types of resting, you can choose to simply recover as quickly as possible or take some extra time to take downtime.

### **Quick Rest (6 hours)**

- When resting the party replenishes all consumables that they have unlocked through campcraft and also any gear that might have been broken or lost is replaced (such as lanterns/ropes etc)
- When resting, you recover two points of Reaper’s Marks.

### **Downtime Rest (12 hours)**

- Includes everything in Quick Rest
- You can take 2 downtime actions

### **Rest (In a Safe Haven)**

When resting in a Safe Haven, like a town or anywhere in civilization that has supplies, the party can spend their hard earned wealth to unlock the campcraft Mastery tree and acquire new equipment. Resting in a Safe Haven also provides all the benefits of resting in the wild.

A good number of Downtime activities can only be performed in Safehavens with some options to unlock them to use out in the wild.

**The Director is responsible for deciding when the party is resting in a Safe Haven. **(A party resting in a very hostile city, might not qualify for the Safe Haven benefits).

### **Preparation**

Every time you rest and the party is getting ready to venture out on the next day of adventures, there is a period which is used to prepare for the adventure. Preparation is used to ‘declare’ what the party is preparing. This includes Soulbinding to items, preparing consumables, kitting etc.

### **Duration**

Most effects, abilities or features in Veil have a static duration. The following list covers most used durations:

- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Start of target’s next turn\|Start of target’s next turn]] : This effect lasts until the beginning of your next turn, that means that as you take your turn in the initiative, the effect wears off immediately.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/End of their next turn\|End of their next turn]]: This effect lasts until the end of your next turn, that means the effect ends when you take a new turn and you use all your actions. If you gain an effect on the current turn, with a duration of end of next turn, you only lose the effect on your next turn, not the current.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/End of Encounter\|End of Encounter]]: This effect is meant to be used within an encounter and lasts until the end of the encounter.
- End of Next Combat Encounter: This effect is meant to be used at any time but it ends after you finalize a combat encounter.
- End of Next Rest/Reused: These effects last until you finish a rest or they also end if the effect is used on a different target. These effects end both if the target rests or the user.
- [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Flat Check End\|Flat Check End]] or Flat Check: Some effects might have a conditional duration; this is denoted by a Flat Check. A Flat Check is always made at the end of your turn. When a Flat Check succeeds or fails, the effect might remain or end, depending on the effect being tested. In general, succeeding on a flat check against a ‘bad’ effect means it is removed; succeeding on a flat check against a ‘good’ effect means it remains.**
- Sustained: Sustained effects usually entail that the creature that is using a sustained effect must use an action to activate the effect and keep it running. When Sustain is the only duration, if the effect is not sustained it ends, if sustained has a secondary duration such as End of Encounter, the effect will remain until the end of the encounter.**

All durations that are End of Encounter/Sustained last one minute when used outside of an encounter.

## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power of the Veil\|Power of the Veil]] 

Veil Points allow you to take some agency in moving the needle for the story. They are a meta currency you can use when a need arises. Veil Points are also called VP in short.

### Veil Points Basics

- You gain 1 veil point at the beginning of a session, you cannot have more than one veil point.
- Veil points can be used to use any ability with a normal cooldown to remove the cooldown.
- **Stretch Your Powers**
    - You can use the [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power of the Veil\|Power of the Veil]] to use your powers in an unusual or unexpected way.
    - This stretch has to be something that your abilities cannot currently do but is within limits what is achievable in the world.
        - Stretch Your Powers cannot be used in [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]]
    - The [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Director\|Director]] decides the outcome.
- **Deus Ex Machina**
    - When you meet an unforeseen event that you might be unprepared for, using the[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Power of the Veil\|Power of the Veil]] might allow you to alter the story in your favor
        - e.g. You wanted to sneak in a mansion, when you show up, the mansions has several patrols. You can use Deux Ex Machina, to say that you would have tried to bribe (skill check) some of the patrols to not show up for work that day.
        - e.g.. You need to find a specific piece of information in a city you know, you can spend a veil point so attempt assuming you know someone (skill check) and can convince them to help.
        - e.g.. You were tasked with exploring a dungeon for a small village and getting rid of some spiders within. When you arrive at the dungeon you realize the entrance is a deep well but you have no rope. In this case, you can spend a veil point to state that you could have asked the villagers and they would have indicated that rope was needed to access the dungeon. So in this case you would have brought a rope with you.
- **Will into existence**
    - if there is a question whether something reasonable exists in the world, the players can make it happen by spending a veil point.
        - For example, the party is escaping and need to jump off a rooftop, they can use the will into existence to have a bale of loose and soft hay on the floor to soften the landing.
        - Example 2: A barred home, are there windows that are open, spending a veil point here can make sure there is one.
- Devil’s Bargain
    - When a player wants to use a Veil Point but does not have any, they can ask for a Devil’s Bargain, where the Director may or may not grant a Veil Point. If the Veil point is granted there is always a cost.
        - The cost usually is usually a [[Complications\|Complications]] or a contract where the player cannot say no to the director.

# **Light and Darkness**

Within the worlds of Veil, the darkness is a veritable danger. Walking around at night makes without light will make everything spookier and concealed to you.

### Light

All light sources in veil have a range which is calculated as an emanation from the source. As long as light is present all creatures that do not have special vision just see normally. Each source of light then extends to the same range as Dim Light. (e.g.. a light of range 6, will have a range 12 dim light, or 6 and 6). Within Dim Light everything is **concealed.**

### Darkness

Darkness is the lack of light but can also be a magical darkness which overlaps with lights and absorbs it making a light source useless.

When you make any searching or vision based Perception checks in **Dim Light, **all results are 1 degree lower as they would be in light.

When you are in **Total Darkness, **you cannot see and are considered **Blind**.

# **Loot and Treasure**

When travelling around the world you will find all sorts of treasure in the strangest of places. Most loot is unnamed and counted in bulk as wealth. Whether you’re skinning a dragon for its scales or plucking rubies from old statues. Their value in wealth is what matters, as it’s an abstraction.

Loot in Veil is meant to mostly be random with chosen hand placed items where applicable. This allows you to find random things but also provides a firm and just hand that provides everyone to find the right items, some of the time.

### **Crafting (Optional)**

Crafting in Veil is optional and based on the setting and adventure type. Crafting projects are used to make magical gear rather rather than relying on random loot. Crafting has three main phases, obtaining a blueprint or recipe, progress and crafting and finishing the item.

Crafting is not for the faint of heart and can take a very long time from start to finish, and also be expensive.

When you want to create an item you need to find or buy a blueprint or recipe which allows you to start your project. Creation of magical gear is hard work and takes many days of labor. The rarer the items, usually the harder it is to make. Most merchants will not part with a precious blueprint easily.

The blueprint defines how many crafting points the project needs to be finished and what sorts of equipment (usually kits or other specific items) and skills are necessary to complete items. Crafting an item is also costly and the blueprint will list what materials are necessary. Some items might require **specific items to be made, this is defined by the Director.** These could be monster parts or items that require finding, or quest completion.

- **Each time** you take the Downtime Activity,** you pay 1 wealth.**
- If an item you’re crafting requires a specific item, you can still progress the crafting but you **won’t be able to finish the crafting.**
- Use a Downtime Activity to craft the item and move one of the Progress Tracks. Each Skill check moves the skill specific Progress Track. Some items might require 2 or more skills to craft.
- Items have a Crafting DC. When you work on your project, you roll a skill check. Any number above the DC is added to the crafting project as progress.
    - Example: if an Item needs 70 Tinkering and 40 Intuition and has a 10 crafting DC. You can make a Tinkering check, if you roll a 15, you progress your project with 5. You need to finish both Skill progress to be able to finish the project.
- Results
    - Critical Success: You add 10 to your Progress.
    - Success: You gain progress equal to the difference between the DC and your roll.
    - Failure: No progress.
    - Critical Failure: You receive a setback, and lose 5 progress.

- Once all progress is finished, you get the item.
