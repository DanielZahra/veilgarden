# [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]]

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] treats time into more malleable chunks rather than looking into exactly what everyone does every second. During [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] the players can choose what their characters are doing as they move around, explore, search, scout, sneak, etc. 

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] is used when the party is performing rituals, exploring dungeons, or doing other activities. In general, most ‘quick’ activities take around 10 minutes, while some others can take hours or days. Available activities are found in [[Exploration Activities\|Exploration Activities]].

---

[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] is mainly split into 3 gameplay loops: Passive Activities, Active Activities, and Dungeon Turns. Dungeon turns are segments of time that pass as you explore. The passive actions chosen by you, are what you’re doing as time passes. This includes how fast or carefully you move, if you’re looking for clues, traps, secrets, or enemies lying in wait to ambush you.

The [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]] active party is used when you or allies switch from something passive to doing something more ‘active.’ Using actions does not require leaving passive mode, as they are quick. However, using rituals or active exploration activities goes into the active state. When you’re taking an active exploration activities you (and the rest of the party) must declare what you’re doing for the next 10 minutes. You can remain in passive mode if you’re not changing what you’re doing. 

---

[[Exploration Activities\|Exploration Activities]] are the most common activities used when you are travelling both in a dangerous spot or a road well travelled. Activities are split into 3 categories, Movement and Passive which are states in which you declare what you are doing ahead of time. And active is where you declare what you are doing now, usually reacting to the environment or interaction.

- **Movement**: Describe how you are moving, this defines the speed, noise, visibility etc., the party always moves at the rate of the slowest party member unless they want to leave them behind.
- **Passive**: Describe what you are doing while exploring, such as detecting mana, covering tracks, following tracks, keeping an eye out for danger, mapping and navigating etc.
- **Active:** Describe when you actively take action based on a finding, environmental effect or interaction during your exploration.

When you move from exploration to [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Encounter Mode\|Encounter Mode]], the exploration activities being performed are considered to be what you are doing.

**During ****[[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Exploration Mode\|Exploration Mode]]****  you can choose an Exploration Activity which is passive. You as a party can choose a single Movement exploration activity as a group which costs no Exploration Activities, as long as you stay as a group. If a single member wants to use a particular Movement Exploration Activity which is different from the rest of the party, it takes one of their Exploration Activities.

If the party is performing an activity and the game moves into encounter mode, activities can still be resumed, the Director is responsible for measuring how many rounds it takes to perform the activity and how many characters need to devote a number of actions to progress the task.

Take a break is an activity where the party takes a 10 minute break when adventuring to quickly recover their ability or item cooldowns and heal to maximum Endurance.

### **10 Minute Turn (Active Exploration)**

When exploring a corridor, a room, reading glyphs on a wall, you should consider that most things that are active exploration activities take around 10 minutes, unless they have a specific duration or time taken. Any ‘current’ exploration activity (passive) you are using is taken automatically.

---

## Doubling Up

When you and an ally both take the same activity, you both make separate rolls and the highest results counts for the whole party.

## Backtracking

When backtracking to previous location you can decide to simply walk rather than explore to speed things up.

### **Push Your Luck aka ‘Luck’**

Exploration needs stakes. If you have infinite time, you can search every tile, pick every lock perfectly, and rest whenever you want. But in **Veil**, time is not on your side. To track the passage of time and the rising danger of your environment, Veil tests your luck against the world, when Luck is not on your side bad things happen.

### How You Use It

Whenever you are exploring, whether delving into a ruin, investigating a crime scene, or trekking through the wilds, your choices dictate the flow of your Luck. The Luck die starts at d20, and every time it is rolled, it degrades the die size by 1. (d20 > d12 >d10 > d8 > d6 >d4 >d2).

When you roll a natural 1, a complication happens and your Luck die goes back to a d20.

### Spending Time

Whenever you commit to an action that is extremely slow, very complicated, or too methodical, you roll your luck.

- *Examples:* Searching a room for loot, taking 10 to rest, or repair items, carefully picking a lock, critically failing some checks which complicate things or ritually identifying a magic item
- **The Rule of Thumb:** Most actions or activities that take 10 or more minutes to perform** roll a luck die.**

### Recklessness and Failure

Whenever you commit to an action that is very loud, rushed, or risky to save time or possibly critically fail a check, you make your luck roll with **disadvantage.** That is you roll two Luck dice simultaneously and take the worse result.

- *Examples:* Smashing a door down instead of picking the lock, shouting an argument in a quiet dungeon, or sprinting through an unknown hallway.
- *Note:* If the pool is empty and you act recklessly, the Director picks up 1 die and rolls it

### Extreme Risks

In some cases when you perform something extremely risky, such as sleeping/resting inside a dangerous area, creating a massive echo in a cave filled with monsters, etc, your luck dice becomes a d2 and is rolled immediately.

### Tipping Point

When a complication happens, a 1 is rolled on any die size, the saving grace is that you go back to having a d20 luck die.

### Complications

When a 1 is rolled you get a **Complication.**

**What is a Complication?** A Complication is rarely a big fight. It means the situation has shifted against you because you took too long or made too much noise.

- **Resources:** Some of your gear breaks
- **Environment:** A path collapses, fog obscures your vision, or a door jams.
- **Threats:** A patrol spots you, a monster wakes up, or your quarry escapes.

Complications are split into, Trivial, Minor, Major and Extreme and rolled on a 2d10 with the extremities being much rarer. In some cases the Director can choose the appropriate complication based on the situation.
