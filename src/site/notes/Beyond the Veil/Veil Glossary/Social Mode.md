## [[Beyond the Veil/Veil Glossary/Social Mode\|Social Mode]] 

**Social Mode** runs tense negotiations, interrogations, debates, and court deals, battles of _words rather than swords._ There is **no initiative**, **no 3-action economy**, and **no reactions**. Instead, the table plays in **free-flowing exchanges**: you speak when it makes sense, and the Director keeps the spotlight moving so both sides trade meaningful “moves.”

You’ll still use **Veil’s four degrees of success** and push two visible clocks: **Favor** (toward winning what you want) and **Backlash** (toward things going badly). **The majority of checks in Social Mode are Secret checks.**

### **The Four Affinities**

All NPCs will have some affinity that they react better to, and all attempts you make uses one of these **affinities** (the opposition is stronger vs some, weaker vs others):

1. **Deception** - misdirection, lies, trap questions, contradictions.
2. **Intimidation** - aggression, violence, pressure, deadlines, implied force.
3. **Diplomacy** - cunning, street smarts, terms, concessions, strategy, legalese.
4. **Charm** - persuasion, rapport, empathy, reputation, flattery.

Each approach has its **own DC** for the opponent. You can discover this during play .

### **Skills and Knowledge in Social Mode**

During social mode, when you are describing your behavior and actions, the skill roll you make is based on the action described. The following table gives an indication how each skill can be used in social mode. In cases where your knowledge is applicable you can make a knowledge roll rather than a skill check. (This usually has a better impact when you have specialized knowledge about a topic).

During a social encounter, you can only use a skill once. When continuing the conversation you can let other party members take over or change your strategy. If you fail to use another skill, it simply counts as a failure.

### **When to use Social Mode?**

If one roll would settle it, the Director just calls for that check. Social Mode is useful when a group of creatures are in a conversation, this includes the whole party. When the outcome is uncertain, high-stakes, or needs back-and-forth, the Director announces **Social Mode** and reveals:

- The **Favor** clock, and
- The **Backlash** clock.
- The scene **immediately ends** when either clock fills.

**Encounter-limited effects** treat the whole Social Mode as **one encounter/scene**.

### **Favor & Backlash**

Every social skill check you roll advances one of the two clocks:

- **Critical Success:** **+2 Favor**
- **Success:** **+1 Favor**
- **Failure:** **+1 Backlash**
- **Critical Failure:** **+2 Backlash**

### **Help Action and Social Mode**

The [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Help\|Help]] action in Social Mode cannot be used in the same way as Exploration since conversation usually happens in real time. Instead the [[Notion/Universal Actions, Mastery Actions and Activities/Universal  Actions/Help\|Help]] action can be invoked by specific features or abilities.

### **Close the Veil**

When Social Mode is in play the Director will usually ‘Close the Veil’, this usually entails that player chatter should be down to a minimum and cross talking (meta-discussions) should not be a thing unless a skill check is being made or an ability is used.
